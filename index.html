Awareness

Tuberculosis (TB)

TB is a disease that can be prevented and treated, yet it claims the lives of over 600 children under 15 every day, with most of these deaths occurring among children under 5.
Causes: TB is caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis, which is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.
Symptoms: Common symptoms of TB include coughing, chest pain, and coughing up blood. Awareness and
Prevention: Countries have developed methods for TB prevention, surveillance, diagnosis, and treatment in adults, but children are still falling through the cracks. Increased awareness and efforts to prevent and treat TB in children are necessary.

Malaria

Malaria is a life-threatening disease that is particularly deadly for young children. According to UNICEF, in 2021, there were 247 million malaria cases globally, resulting in 619,000 deaths, with 77% of these deaths being children under 5 years old.
Causes: Malaria is caused by the bite of an infected mosquito.
Symptoms: Common symptoms of malaria include fever, chills, flu-like symptoms, and in severe cases, coma and death.
Awareness and Prevention: Insecticide-treated mosquito nets are an effective defense against malaria. Swift diagnosis and treatment are also crucial in preventing the spread of the disease.
